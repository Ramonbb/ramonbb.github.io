Projects

Current and past research lines in tourism management, marketing, service quality, and digital consumer evidence.

Cruise Tourism Research

Description: Study of cruise passenger reviews and destination experiences across ports, onboard services, and itinerary attributes.

Methods used: Text Mining, LDA Topic Modeling, Sentiment Analysis, Cluster Analysis.

Key findings: Placeholder findings indicate that service reliability, itinerary design, food quality, and staff interaction strongly shape cruise satisfaction.

Related publications: Sentiment and Topic Modeling of Cruise Tourism Reviews.

Airline Service Quality Research

Description: Analysis of passenger evaluations of airline service quality using online reviews and structured ratings.

Methods used: Sentiment Analysis, CHAID, Quantitative Research Methods.

Key findings: Placeholder findings suggest that punctuality, cabin comfort, staff behavior, and complaint handling are central to perceived quality.

Related publications: Service Quality Dimensions in Online Tourism Platforms.

Project link

Urban Destination Attractiveness

Description: Research on how visitors describe and evaluate urban tourism destinations in online platforms.

Methods used: Topic Modeling, Text Mining, Structural Equation Modeling.

Key findings: Placeholder findings highlight cultural attractions, walkability, gastronomy, safety, and authenticity as recurring attractiveness drivers.

Related publications: Online Reviews and the Formation of Tourism Experience Quality.

Project link

Heritage Hotels and Paradores

Description: Investigation of accommodation experiences in heritage hotels and Paradores, with attention to authenticity and service design.

Methods used: Text Mining, Sentiment Analysis, Cluster Analysis.

Key findings: Placeholder findings point to the combined importance of historic atmosphere, service professionalism, location, and dining experiences.

Related publications: Heritage Hotel Experiences Through Online Guest Reviews.

Project link

Residents and Tourism in Seville

Description: Study of resident perceptions of tourism impacts, urban pressure, economic benefits, and sustainable management in Seville.

Methods used: CHAID, Cluster Analysis, Quantitative Research Methods.

Key findings: Placeholder findings identify distinct resident segments with different tolerance levels, benefit perceptions, and policy preferences.

Related publications: Residents' Attitudes Toward Tourism Development in Seville.
